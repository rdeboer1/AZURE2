Detailed Description

An AZURE combination.

In the differential cross section formula of R-Matrix, nested inside the sum is a sum over . In the language of AZURE, these are equivalent to combinations of two reaction pathways. If the pathways are the same, the term represents the actual contribution from the pathway to the cross section. If they are different, the term represents the interference between the two.

The pathways combination is created specifically using references to two positions in the MGroup and ECMGroup vectors under the corresponding KGroup object. Additionally, the coefficients are passed along with the interference type. The interference type is either RR, ER, RE,or EE, indicating which vector, the MGroup or ECMGroup, the stored indices refer to.
